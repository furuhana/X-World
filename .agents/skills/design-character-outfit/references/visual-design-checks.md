# Visual Design Checks

Review the completed outfit as a game or animation character. Revise the design to solve any failed check.

## Readability

- Can the audience infer the character's broad identity and attitude at first glance?
- Does the silhouette remain distinct when viewed small?
- Is there one dominant focal point and a clear secondary hierarchy?
- Is at least one feature memorable without relying on backstory?

## Professional Readability

- Is the occupation real, specific, and explainable?
- Can a player identify the broad professional field from the major silhouette, garment structures, and color arrangement?
- If badges, documents, and small tools were removed, would the outfit still communicate the broad professional field?
- Did the design compare multiple professional visual sources before deriving the core visual element?
- Did the design distinguish broad professional visual sources from the finished core visual element?
- Are real occupational tools and identifiers clarifying the specific occupation rather than carrying all readability?
- Has the outfit degraded into generic modern workwear, a generic suit, a generic jacket, or generic tactical clothing?
- Is the design overly literal and ordinary because it prioritizes workplace accuracy above game readability?
- Does the design avoid copying a completed existing character or stock costume?

## Core Element and Deployment

- Is there one primary core visual element and at most one supporting element?
- Is the primary element concrete enough to describe through a standard form and recognition anchors?
- Does it remain recognizable through more than one allowed transformation?
- Does the design use at least two different application methods rather than repeatedly copying the complete motif?
- Do element applications suit the construction or function of their locations?
- Does at least one major element application combine decoration with storage, protection, adjustment, fastening, mounting, reinforcement, or wearing comfort?
- Does every claimed practical application answer an established character need rather than imitate generic utility or tactical styling?
- Has the design avoided arbitrary element-shaped holes in single-layer garments?
- Does every unusual opening have a clear access, ventilation, movement, adjustment, closure, or layered-construction purpose?
- Are unusual opening edges visibly reinforced, finished, secured, or connected to an underlayer?
- Is there one clear primary expression with subordinate secondary and accent expressions?
- Do the upper and lower body share a deliberate visual balance rather than compete?
- Are details clustered intentionally instead of distributed evenly?
- Are there sufficient quiet areas near strong element applications?
- If small embroidery, badges, tools, and hanging ornaments were removed, would the core element still organize the outfit?
- Did the design actively derive accessories, carried objects, and wearing relationships rather than treating them only as optional additions?
- Did candidate generation include a personal object, a wearing-or-carrying relationship object, and a large silhouette-changing object?
- Did candidate generation explore at least three different body regions or wearing relationships?
- Does each selected accessory have a credible source, wearing relationship, or clear visual responsibility?
- Does each accessory contribute a missing role, life trace, relationship, or silhouette value rather than duplicate garment decoration?
- Does the final selection avoid defaulting to waist-mounted small objects without a character-specific reason?
- Did regional exploration remain candidate exploration rather than forcing an object into every explored body region?

## Body and Clothing

- Is the character built on an enlarged heavyweight skeletal frame rather than a realistic or lean frame with added muscle?
- Is the waist moderately broad and powerful without becoming a smooth oversized belly?
- Does the abdomen project because of six enormous raised abdominal blocks rather than a continuous round volume?
- Are the chest and all six abdominal blocks clearly readable through the stretched inner layer?
- Is the head slightly enlarged and proportionally substantial, with a broad cranial shape and full back-of-head volume?
- Is the face broad and square, with wide cheekbones, a heavy wide jaw, and a short wide chin?
- Does the body appear heroic through mass and width rather than through shrinking the head?
- Do collars, scarves, hoods, lapels, or trapezius forms leave the head silhouette clearly readable?
- Are the neck, joints, hands, feet, arms, and legs all proportionally massive?
- Do the heroic proportions appear deliberately larger than a realistic human?
- Does the clothing support the intended body emphasis?
- Do fit, closures, and materials respond plausibly to the body volume?
- Does the main chest-and-abdomen mass remain readable?
- When necklaces, cross-body straps, harnesses, suspended objects, cape fasteners, or chest connectors cross the torso, do they form intentional wearing relationships without meaninglessly concealing the whole torso?
- Does any tailoring, long narrow garment, or trouser cut make the character appear lean, elongated, or narrow-waisted? If so, revise it.
- With low-cut or ankle-height footwear, do full-length trouser hems stop slightly above the shoe openings, reveal a narrow band of white socks, and leave the footwear unobscured?
- Does the trouser design preserve freedom in leg width, hem opening, cuff construction, and taper instead of defaulting to jogger-style hems?
- Can the character perform the required actions?
- Does each hand have at most one clear action, including expressive or stabilizing gestures?
- Have unrelated handheld occupational objects been stored, relocated, or omitted when the hands are needed for the current action?
- Are gloves and other normally paired wearable items symmetric unless the design visibly establishes which side differs and why?

## Equipment and Density

- Do carried items have plausible locations and access paths?
- What aspect of the character, activity, environment, relationship, or composition produced each selected object?
- Why is each object worn, carried, tied, hung, draped, stored, or held?
- Does each object have a plausible visible attachment, connection, support, movement behavior, or access path?
- Does each object have exactly one unambiguous current state: in active use, hand-carried, worn, attached for access, or stored?
- Can the design explain what supports each object's weight, what secures it, and how it is accessed?
- Does each object create a meaningful wearing relationship, life trace, silhouette change, identity cue, or compositional value?
- Do garments, accessories, weapons, and mounts physically conflict?
- Were shoulder, back, large handheld, and multi-region objects evaluated as candidates rather than rejected early because they change the silhouette?
- Are optional details clustered intentionally rather than evenly scattered?
- Are secondary details competing with the face, torso, or signature feature?
- Does another object already perform the same role?
- Does each object's visual weight match its design responsibility?
- Would removing an item lose identity information, character specificity, structural value, wearing relationships, silhouette value, or compositional value?
- If removing an item improves clarity, is that improvement greater than the character or structural value lost?
- Are wrists limited to lightweight close-fitting wrist-worn objects rather than suspended notebooks, memo pads, bags, or tool pouches?
- Are forearms and upper arms free of hanging bags, tool kits, books, and other loose heavy objects?
- Are watches and other body-specific functional objects worn at their intended body location rather than arbitrarily attached to bag straps?
- Are compact frequently accessed tools placed at a credible waist, hip, or fitted storage location when appropriate?
- Are medium and large document cases, tool cases, and bags supported by credible cross-body, shoulder, back, or equivalent load-distributing structures?

## Chest Objects and High-Visual-Weight Ornaments

- Does every chest object have a credible source or clear visual responsibility?
- If a chest object fastens, closes, connects, supports, carries, or counterweights something, is the load-bearing relationship visible?
- Is every large, bright, luminous, precious-looking, or strongly contrasting central chest object treated as a primary visual element?
- Does such an object have a visible attachment or support structure rather than floating on the torso?
- Does it relate to garment construction, wearing relationships, equipment, culture, identity, history, ritual, or composition?
- Does its visual weight match the character and overall style?
- Does it have at least one structural echo and one restrained visual echo?
- Would removing it materially weaken structure, identity readability, or composition?
- Are gems, ornate brooches, large metal ornaments, and luminous cores used as low-probability candidates rather than routine decoration?
- For ordinary or everyday characters, were lived-in personal objects and plausible carried items considered before high-visual-weight ornaments?
- Does the design avoid mechanically repeating the same gem, emblem, or ornament merely to create echoes?

## Two-Dimensional Finish

- Are characters and clothing rendered as clean two-dimensional color shapes and cel shadows?
- Are surface texture patterns and realistic micro-details absent?
- When smoothness, polish, gloss, oiliness, or reflection is used, is it expressed through simplified cel-highlight and reflection shapes rather than 3D or photorealistic materials?

## Final Revision

Reject and rebuild any accessory selection that skipped active derivation, forces objects into implausible locations for region variety, defaults every small object to the waist without reason, removes all meaningful objects merely because they are nonessential, or uses a conspicuous chest object without a credible source and visible attachment. Reject objects with ambiguous current states, unsupported weight, implausible access, unexplained suspension from the wrist or arm, or arbitrary relocation of body-specific functional items. Reject unexplained single gloves and any hand carrying an unrelated object while needed for the current action. Fix conflicts and remove unsupported or redundant objects while retaining meaningful accessories and carried items.

Reject and rebuild any occupational design that depends entirely on badges or small tools for professional readability, defaults to visually anonymous modern workwear, or combines unrelated motifs into a costume collage. Reject any element system that remains only a broad theme, copies the complete symbol across every garment, gives every body region equal visual weight, leaves no quiet areas, or cuts arbitrary element-shaped holes into single-layer garments. Reject unusual openings without a practical purpose and visible construction logic. Reject any result that reads as a lean muscular man, conventional fitness model, realistic-height adult, small-headed or narrow-faced anime man, sharply tapered body, obese man, smooth-bellied strongman, or body-fat-dominant build. Reject any tight inner layer that hides the six-block abdominal contour, and reject collars or trapezius forms that visually swallow the head. Fix conflicts and remove weak elements before output. Mention only the most important changes and intentional omissions in `【设计复核与删减】`.
